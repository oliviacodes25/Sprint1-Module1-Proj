    • Making It With Switchfoot

    Jon Foreman became famous because of the band Switchfoot, where he was lead singer and co-founder. Their style is rock. They are not Christian per say, but there is an element of faith in their songs. One of their stepping stones to fame includes a song "Learning to Breathe" that was featured in the Mandy Moore film "A Walk to Remember."

    Unique Characteristics of the Band

    An interesting thing about Jon Foreman and Switchfoot is they have an unconventional style and outlook. They also came together as a band through surfing. Jon sums it up this way: "We all love to surf and have been surfing all our lives so to us, the name made sense. To switch your feet means to take a new stance facing the opposite direction. It's about change and movement, a different way of approaching life and music." (Wikipedia)

    Another interesting thing about their music is that they are not overtly Christian, but their songs have deep spiritual meaning. Jon Foreman is extremely honest in his music, and in real life he went through a faith crisis. An example of his vulnerability as he wrestled through his faith is a song called "Jesus I Have My Doubts," from one of his solo albums.

    Jon Foreman Today

    Since the early 2000s with Switchfoot Jon Foreman has branched off into a solo career and has created many albums, including "Departures" which is a portrayal of his faith and his doubts and ultimately his belief in Christ. As for his life today, Jon is married, and has a 11 year old daughter and a 5 year old son.
